# 🥗 Diet Guide Feature

## Overview
A comprehensive food categories and diet chart to help diabetes patients make informed food choices. The guide categorizes foods into "Good", "Moderate", and "Avoid" based on their glycemic index and nutritional value.

## Food Highlights

### Best Foods for Diabetes
1. **Bitter Gourd (Karela)** - Natural blood sugar reducer
2. **Spinach** - Low carb, high fiber, rich in iron
3. **Berries** - Low GI, high antioxidants
4. **Oats (Steel-cut)** - High fiber, stabilizes blood sugar
5. **Almonds** - Low GI, healthy fats
6. **Fish (Salmon)** - Omega-3, heart healthy
7. **Lentils (Dal)** - Plant protein, high fiber
8. **Green Tea** - May improve insulin sensitivity

### Foods to Avoid
1. **Soda/Soft Drinks** - Very high sugar
2. **White Bread** - High GI, rapid sugar spike
3. **Dried Fruits** - Concentrated sugar
4. **Fruit Juices** - No fiber, rapid absorption
5. **Chips/Crisps** - High carb, fried
6. **Cookies/Biscuits** - High sugar, refined flour
7. **Potatoes** - High GI, spikes blood sugar
8. **Candy/Sweets** - Pure sugar

### Moderate Foods (Portion Control)
1. **Banana** - Eat half at a time
2. **White Rice** - Limit to 1/2 cup, pair with vegetables
3. **Carrots** - Eat raw, moderate GI when cooked
4. **Cashews** - Higher carb than other nuts
5. **Dark Chocolate** - 70%+ cocoa, small piece only

## Tips for Success

### Meal Planning
- Fill 1/2 plate with vegetables
- 1/4 plate with protein
- 1/4 plate with whole grains
- Add a small portion of healthy fats

### Snacking Smart
- Keep good snacks handy (nuts, vegetables)
- Avoid packaged snacks
- Pair carbs with protein (apple + peanut butter)

### Eating Out
- Choose grilled over fried
- Ask for dressings on the side
- Skip the bread basket
- Choose water or unsweetened beverages

### Cooking Tips
- Use healthy cooking methods (steaming, grilling, baking)
- Limit oil and ghee
- Use herbs and spices instead of salt
- Cook vegetables al dente (slightly firm)

## Glycemic Index (GI) Guide

### Low GI (0-55) - Best Choice
- Slow digestion, gradual blood sugar rise
- Examples: Most vegetables, nuts, legumes

### Medium GI (56-69) - Moderate
- Moderate blood sugar impact
- Examples: Whole wheat, brown rice, some fruits

### High GI (70+) - Avoid
- Rapid blood sugar spike
- Examples: White bread, white rice, sugary foods

## Portion Sizes

### Vegetables
- Unlimited non-starchy vegetables
- 1/2 cup cooked starchy vegetables

### Fruits
- 1 small fruit or 1/2 cup
- Berries: 3/4 to 1 cup

### Grains
- 1/2 cup cooked rice or pasta
- 1 slice bread or 1 small roti

### Proteins
- 3-4 oz (palm-sized portion)
- 1/2 cup cooked legumes

### Nuts
- 1 oz (small handful, about 23 almonds)

## Special Considerations

### For Type 1 Diabetes
- Count carbohydrates carefully
- Match insulin to carb intake
- Keep fast-acting carbs for lows

### For Type 2 Diabetes
- Focus on weight management
- Emphasize low-GI foods
- Regular meal timing

### For Prediabetes
- Prevent progression with diet
- Increase fiber intake
- Reduce processed foods
